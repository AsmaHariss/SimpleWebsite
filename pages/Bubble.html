Why so special?

The Bubble Nebula, also known as NGC 7635, is a sphere of active star formation glowing
faintly in the constellation Cassiopeia. The hydrogen gas cloud from which the stars form
emits red light, the characteristic color of hydrogen, by absorbing energy from them.




Can we see Bubble Nebula from Earth?

With an 8-or-10-inch (200 or 250 mm) telescope, the nebula is visible as an extremely
faint and large shell around the star. The nearby 7th magnitude star on the west hinders
observation, but one can view the nebula using averted vision.




How do you find the Bubble Nebula?

The Bubble Nebula is an emission nebula located 7,100 – 11,000 light-years away in the
northern constellation Cassiopeia. With an apparent magnitude of 10, it can be observed
in medium and larger telescopes. The H II region appears close to the bright open cluster
Messier 52 in the sky.
