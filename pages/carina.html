Why so special?

The Carina Nebula, NGC 3372, is an enormous cloud of gas and dust home to several
massive and bright stars, including at least a dozen that are 50 to 100 times the
mass of our Sun. It is an emission nebula, meaning that the intense radiation from
its stars ionizes the gas and causes it to glow.



Can we see Carina Nebula from Earth?

The Carina nebula can only be seen from the southern hemisphere



Does the Carina Nebula still exist?

The Carina Nebula is an immense cloud of gas and dust where a maelstrom
of star birth and death is taking place. The nebula is located an estimated
7,500 light-years away in the southern constellation Carina the Keel
(part of the older, larger southern constellation Argo Navis, the ship of Jason
and the Argonauts).
